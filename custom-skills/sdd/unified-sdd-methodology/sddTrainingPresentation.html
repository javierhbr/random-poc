Welcome to SDD

Have you ever asked a team to build a "user profile page," only to get something completely different weeks later?

Spec-Driven Development (SDD) fixes this. It's a simple, step-by-step way to ensure everyone agrees on exactly what we are building before anyone figures out how to build it.

The Core Idea

Think of SDD like building a house. You don't just hand a builder some bricks and say "build a house." You start with a blueprint, agree on the rooms, and define the rules. SDD is our blueprint process for software.

Adoption Strategy

How We Adopt SDD

We don't expect teams to perfectly execute all 5 phases on Day 1. Instead, we roll out this methodology in Two Iterations to make adoption safe and steady.

1

Iteration 1: Learn to Define Work

Focus only on the first three phases: Platform ➔ Assess ➔ Specify.

Goal: Stop guessing what to build. We don't change how engineering works yet; we simply ensure specs are crystal clear, boundaries are known, and glossaries are used before anyone starts planning.

2

Iteration 2: Learn to Deliver Work

Add the final two phases: Plan ➔ Deliver.

Goal: Once Specification is strong, we apply that rigor to engineering execution. We implement phased tasks, strict PRs, verification evidence, and formal archiving.

Key Artifacts Generated (Using OpenSpec):

proposal.md

Defines the problem, goals, and strictly separates Non-Goals to prevent the project from expanding later. Must only use terms found in the Shared Glossary.

Captures behavioral changes using ADDED, MODIFIED, and REMOVED sections. It shows exactly what is changing compared to reality today, making it testable.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the "Hard Boundary" Rule for tools?

Think of it like City Hall vs. Your House.

At City Hall (Phase 1 & 2 Platform work), we use multiple AI tools (BMAD, Speckit) to brainstorm and set big shared rules. But when you go to Your House to actually build your specific feature (Phase 3+ Component work), you are only allowed to use OpenSpec. This keeps your workspace clean, focused, and free from conflicting AI advice.

What if I don't know if my feature will break another team's system?

You don't have to guess! In Phase 1, the Architect creates a Dependency Map. It tells you exactly how systems are connected using "Impact Tiers" (e.g., Tier 1 means systems must change together). The Team Lead will check this map during Phase 2 before you even start writing your spec.

Why can't I just use my own words in the Proposal?

Because words are dangerous! If you ask for "Validation," you might mean a spellcheck, but the engineer might build a database verification tool. You must use the Shared Glossary so everyone builds exactly what is intended. If your word isn't in the glossary, add it there first!

Do we have to do all 5 phases perfectly on day one?

No. We use a Two Iteration Rollout. In Iteration 1, we only focus on getting the first three phases right (Platform ➔ Assess ➔ Specify). We just want to stop guessing what to build. Only after we master that do we move to Iteration 2 to change how the engineers Plan and Deliver.

You're Ready to Go!

SDD helps us move faster by avoiding misunderstandings. Let's recap your role:

Wait for the green light. Don't start writing specs until the Team Lead finishes Phase 2 (Assess) and confirms who owns the change.

Check the Glossary. Ensure every term in your proposal is defined. If it's not, define it with the team.

Write Delta Specs. Be clear about what is ADDED, MODIFIED, and REMOVED. Don't worry about the "how."

Trust the Process. Once you hand off to Phase 4 & 5, trust the Architect to plan safely and the Team Lead to deliver the slices.
